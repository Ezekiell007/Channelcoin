This document defines how decisions are made within the **channelGold (CHG)** project and how the community participates in shaping the future of the ecosystem.

---

## 1. Purpose of Governance

The goal of the governance system is to:

- Promote transparency  
- Involve the community in decision-making  
- Ensure fair and decentralized development  
- Align all changes with the long-term vision of CHG  

---

## 2. Voting Power

Voting power is based on the amount of **CHG tokens** held by a participant.

- More tokens = greater voting influence  
- Votes are counted transparently  
- Snapshot of holdings may be taken before each vote  

---

## 3. Governance Types

### **A. Community Voting**
Token holders vote on topics such as:

- Feature proposals  
- Staking upgrades  
- Economic adjustments  
- FTFS updates  
- Partnerships  
- Treasury allocation  

### **B. Team Governance**
The core team may vote on:

- Security fixes  
- Emergency decisions  
- Technical implementations  
- High-risk updates  

---

## 4. Proposal Process

Anyone may submit a proposal by following these steps:

1. Open a **Governance Proposal (GP)** issue in the repository  
2. Describe the purpose, benefits, and risks  
3. Wait for review by the team  
4. Community voting will be scheduled if approved  

---

## 5. Voting Methods

CHG governance may use one or more methods:

- On-chain voting  
- Off-chain snapshot voting  
- Weighted token voting  
- Simple majority or percentage thresholds  

Voting details will be announced for each proposal.

---

## 6. Transparency

All proposals, discussions, and results will be publicly available through:

- GitHub issues  
- Official Telegram  
- Official announcements  

---

## 7. Governance Updates

This governance model may evolve over time.  
Changes to governance itself will require community approval.

---

## 8. Contact

For governance-related questions, please reach out through official communication channels.
